How to Find a Reliable Jewelry Manufacturer in India for International Brands

By Param Jewellery · April 2026 · 8 min read

India is one of the world's most important jewelry manufacturing hubs. With a deep tradition of goldsmithing, access to skilled artisans, competitive production costs, and a strong export infrastructure, India supplies jewelry to some of the world's most recognised brands. But for designers and retailers new to the sourcing process, finding the right manufacturer can feel overwhelming.

This guide walks you through everything you need to know — from what to look for in a manufacturer, to how the process works, to the questions you must ask before placing your first order.

Why Source Jewelry Manufacturing from India?

India accounts for a significant share of the world's fine jewelry exports. There are several reasons international brands consistently choose Indian manufacturers:

  • Skilled craftsmanship: India has generational expertise in gold and diamond jewelry, with artisans trained in traditional techniques like filigree, meenakari, and kundan, as well as modern CAD/CAM manufacturing.
  • Competitive pricing: Lower labour costs compared to manufacturers in Europe or North America allow brands to achieve higher margins without compromising quality.
  • Wide range of capabilities: Indian manufacturers can work across fine gold jewelry, silver, fashion costume jewelry, gemstone setting, and custom bespoke creations — often within a single facility.
  • Strong export infrastructure: India has well-developed trade corridors to the USA, UK, UAE, EU, and Australia, with established logistics providers and customs frameworks.
  • Certifications and compliance: Leading Indian manufacturers hold certifications like ISO 9001, RJC (Responsible Jewellery Council) membership, BIS hallmarking compliance, and conflict-free diamond sourcing (Kimberley Process).

What to Look for in a Jewelry Manufacturer

Not all manufacturers are equal. When evaluating a potential manufacturing partner, here are the key criteria to assess:

1. Experience and Track Record

Look for manufacturers with a proven history of working with international clients. Ask how many years they have been manufacturing for export, which countries they ship to, and how many pieces they produce monthly. A manufacturer with 15+ years of experience and 500,000+ pieces produced is significantly lower risk than a newer operation.

2. Certifications

For international markets, certifications matter — both for compliance and for your own brand's credibility. Key certifications to look for include:

  • ISO 9001 (quality management systems)
  • RJC (Responsible Jewellery Council) membership
  • BIS / Hallmarking compliance
  • Conflict-Free Diamonds (Kimberley Process certification)
  • REACH / EU Nickel compliance (important for EU market entry)

3. CAD/CAM and Design Capability

If you're bringing original designs to market, your manufacturer needs to be more than a production house — they need to be a capable design partner. Look for in-house CAD design, wax prototyping, and a process that allows for revisions before bulk production begins.

4. Intellectual Property Protection

Design theft is a real concern in the jewelry manufacturing world. Any serious manufacturer will sign an NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) before reviewing your designs. If a manufacturer is reluctant to do this, walk away.

5. Transparent Communication

International manufacturing requires clear, responsive communication. Look for manufacturers who assign a dedicated account manager, provide regular production updates with photos, and respond to queries quickly across time zones.

6. Flexible MOQ

Minimum order quantities (MOQs) vary significantly. For brands just starting out, look for manufacturers that offer low MOQs — ideally 10 pieces per style for fine jewelry, or even 1 piece for bespoke creations. This lets you test designs and market response before committing to large orders.

The Manufacturing Process: What to Expect

Working with an Indian jewelry manufacturer for the first time can feel unfamiliar. Here is a typical step-by-step process:

  1. Quote Request: Submit your designs (sketches, CAD files, or reference images), metal preferences, gemstone requirements, and estimated quantity. Reputable manufacturers respond within 24–48 hours with itemised pricing.
  2. NDA Signing: Before any proprietary files are shared, a Non-Disclosure Agreement is signed to protect your intellectual property.
  3. CAD Design and Approval: The manufacturer's CAD team prepares technical drawings based on your designs. You review, request revisions, and approve before anything is physically made.
  4. Sample / Prototype Production: A physical wax model or metal sample is made and shipped to you for review. This is your chance to check proportions, finish, weight, and stone setting before bulk production begins.
  5. Bulk Manufacturing: Once the sample is approved, full production begins. Expect regular photo updates showing your pieces at each stage — casting, setting, polishing, and quality control.
  6. Quality Control and Export: Every piece passes strict QC. The manufacturer handles hallmarking, packaging, export documentation, and shipping via DHL, FedEx, or bonded carriers.

Pro tip: Always order a sample before placing a bulk order — no matter how confident you feel. Seeing and holding a physical prototype before committing to a large run saves time, money, and potential disappointment.

Questions to Ask Before Placing Your First Order

Use these questions to quickly assess whether a manufacturer is right for you:

  • What is your MOQ for fine jewelry vs fashion jewelry?
  • Do you sign NDAs before I share designs?
  • What is your standard sampling timeline?
  • Do you provide a dedicated account manager?
  • Which certifications do you hold?
  • How do you handle quality issues or defects?
  • Do you handle all export documentation and customs?
  • Can I visit your facility or see a virtual factory tour?
  • Can you provide references from existing international clients?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Choosing on price alone. The cheapest manufacturer is rarely the best. Low pricing often signals corner-cutting on materials, labour, or quality control.
  • Skipping the prototype stage. A sample order is non-negotiable. Design files never capture everything — proportions, weight, and finish must be physically verified.
  • Not signing an NDA. Your designs are your business. Protect them before sharing any files.
  • Ignoring certifications. If you're selling into the EU, USA, or UK, your manufacturer's certifications directly affect your compliance obligations as a brand.
  • Poor communication from the start. If a manufacturer is slow to respond to enquiries before you've placed an order, expect the same during production.
